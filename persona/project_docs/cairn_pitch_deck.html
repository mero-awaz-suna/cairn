The problem

When you're struggling, you're alone at the worst possible time.

Career pressure. Burnout. Anxiety. These hit students and professionals every day — but talking about it still feels like a weakness. And even when people want help, they don't know where to turn.

The gap in existing solutions

Therapy is expensive. Hotlines feel extreme. Support forums are cold and full of strangers.

Most people don't need a diagnosis. They need to feel heard by someone who genuinely gets it — someone who's been in the same storm and found their way out.

"I don't need a doctor. I just need to know someone else has felt this way too — and survived it."
Introducing Cairn

A voice journal that knows when you need people — and finds exactly the right ones.

Cairn is named after the stone markers hikers leave on mountains — signals that say: someone was here before you, and they made it through.

How it works — step by step

Four moments that change everything.

1
You speak, not type. Every day, you record a 60-second voice note — how you're feeling, what's on your mind. No prompts, no forms. Just your voice.
2
Cairn listens and builds your persona. Over time, it quietly understands your stress level, what kind of challenges you face, and where you are in your recovery journey — your "stage."
3
When you press "I need help," a circle forms. Within seconds, Cairn finds 4–6 real people — anonymous — who match your context and are available right now. Your group is carefully balanced so it never becomes a spiral.
4
You leave better than you entered. When the session ends, you get a personal summary of what helped, and your persona updates — so the next match is even better.
The science behind the group

We don't group people by pain. We design groups like ecosystems.

Every circle is deliberately balanced — because a room full of people in crisis only spirals deeper. Instead:

In the storm
1 person — triggered, needs to be heard right now
Finding ground
1–2 people — still raw, but processing and relatable
Through it
1 person — recovered, offers real hope, not advice
Different paths
No two members share the same trauma type

Members are matched on regionlanguagelife stageoccupationstressor type — so conversations feel familiar, not foreign.

New feature — the memory wall

What if no live group is a perfect match right now? New

You press "I need help." Cairn finds you the most relatable live circle available and puts you in it immediately. But it also shows you something else alongside it:

Your live circle

The best available match right now. Real people, online, ready to talk. Anonymous and balanced.

Echoes from past circles

AI-curated summaries of past sessions from people who faced what you're facing. Distilled to positivity only — what helped, what shifted, what gave hope. No raw pain, no trauma details.

So even at 3am when few people are online, you're never truly alone. You have a live group and the collected wisdom of every circle that came before.

What makes this different

Three things nobody else does.

Persona-driven matching

Not keywords or mood tags — a living model of who you are, built from your voice over days and weeks.

Engineered hope

The group composition guarantees someone in the room has been where you are — and found the other side.

The memory wall

Past sessions don't disappear. Their positive essence lives on and becomes support for future strangers.

Safety — built in, not bolted on

Anonymous by default. Safe by design.

A
No permanent identity. Groups dissolve after every session. Nothing is stored about who said what.
B
AI circuit breaker. If sentiment turns harmful, the AI facilitator redirects the conversation — or surfaces a crisis helpline immediately.
C
Positive-only memory wall. Past session summaries are filtered by AI — only insights that helped, nothing that could re-trigger.
